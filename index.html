Creator & Template Ecosystem

MIRO
BEFORE

Templates trickled in through organic submissions with no feedback loop or growth vision. Not a community but a transaction between submitter and platform.

AFTER

Templates are durable organic assets — they live in search, get shared, and drive adoption long after they're published. The strategic bet was to treat creators as partners and build the infrastructure to make that worthwhile for them. Built a community of practice around creation where we reward and incentivize creators. Members inspire and learn from each other. A Creator Fund commissions templates aligned to organic growth goals, closing gaps in high-opportunity search categories and ICP use cases every month.

Growth milestones: 1,500 templates when I started. 3,200 by end of year one. 7,000+ today.

At our AI Workflows launch, 80+ templates went live simultaneously, including this AI enablement example, driving immediate usage and social proof. Most recently, we activated creators for our MCP launch, producing social content and templates to demonstrate new AI use cases.

Indeed Career Guide

INDEED
BEFORE

Organic growth plateauing — approaching the ceiling of job listing optimization. No content addressing career services keywords. Job seekers had unmet informational and emotional needs with no trusted resource.

AFTER

Job seekers needed help navigating their search, not just a list of jobs. That unmet need represented a greenfield of high-intent search keywords Indeed had no content to address. The strategy was to serve the job seeker's full journey and turn content and community into an acquisition engine. Conceived and named the product. Built platform and content strategy from scratch. Milestone-based growth from first article to 1M visits, 20M, then 150M. Made the business case for dedicated product investment. Became Indeed's number one source of new user acquisition globally.
